Integrated Plant Maintenance System

The Integrated Computerized Plant Maintenance System (IPMS) is a state of the art system that automates all the activities performed by the maintenance management department of an organization. It has been designed to provide on-line information management to meet the operational requirements of the Maintenance Management department.
 


Common Code Maintenance

The formulating and freezing of the codification structure is an important milestone in the design of any application, more so in a vast and complex one like the IPMS system. A single table that contains many smaller sub-tables surrounded by many common functionality components is what makes up the Codes Table concept. Each sub-table within the Codes Table provides a method of defining a set of basic elements within the system. The description field may contain either a text description of the code value or a translation value.

Codes tables improve centralization and communication of standard system code values. This module is a repository of all the codes that are used in the IPMS system. Any addition, modification, or deletion to the codes is done through this module.

Equipment Classification

The Equipment Classification code is used to uniquely identify the position of each equipment in the plant. Having a logical codification structure it incorporates the maintenance of the equipment catalogues comprising of the facility (shop), group, equipment, assembly, subassembly and part. This method of classification enables the system to provide equipment information in the form of a "tree" hierarchy thus enabling the user to pinpoint the desired piece of equipment with the utmost precision. Thus, this module is used to classify the equipment classification tree.

The users have the option of entering the details for the equipment as well as capturing the details of equipment manufacturers.

The equipment classification module aids in:

  - dentification of each equipment up to the part level.
- Flexibility of defining Equipment at different levels.
- Tracking of Equipment.
- Tracking of Costs, Warranties, Weights, Vendors, Manufacturers, Model Number, Serial Number, Installation Date, History etc.
- Establishment of Equipment hierarchy.
- Identification of associated Spares leading like BOM
- Establishing relationships between Equipment and drawings.
- dentifying the possible effects of Equipment failure.
 


Rotatable Equipment Tracking

Since a Rotatable Equipment may be installed in any Facility across the Plant, the Rotatable Equipment Tracking module allows for the position of a Rotatable Equipment to be traced and located across the Facilities at any given time. The Equipment is identified by its Type and Tag Number and can be located, throughout the Plant, at any level in the Equipment Classification hierarchy. This is specially designed only for those items which are movable in nature.

The System maintains:

  - The current status and location of each Rotatable Equipment
- The history of all the previous status and locations of each Rotatable Equipment

 

Maintenance Planning

Prior to the creation of work-orders, each maintenance activity needs to be planned. Planning consists, primarily, of defining the jobs and tasks as well as defining and organizing the resources required to carry them out. This module ensures the effective planning of equipment repairs and maintenance. The areas which this module addresses are : Safety Procedures, Maintenance Contracts, Jobs and Tasks, Manpower Plan, Defining the Scheduled Down Turns and Defining the average manpower rates by Facility.

 
 


Maintenance Scheduling and Feedback

The Maintenance Scheduling and Feedback module consists of scheduling the Maintenance Tasks, creating Maintenance Work-Orders and recording Feedback on these Work-Orders without failing to register the Maintenance Delays, if any.

The IPMS System categorizes Maintenance activity to be of two types - one concerned with the routine, daily jobs and the other concerned with all other periodic and one-time jobs. The daily routine Jobs are defined as the Daily Checklist Jobs. As these Jobs are done everyday, they are scheduled by each Facility for a period of time. The System generates a Work-Order for the defined period as well as a Task List for each Task under the Job. The maintenance crew can do the job as per the specified Task list and keep a record of the daily resources consumption required in order to complete the various Tasks. At the end of the specified period, Feedback on this Work-order can be recorded and the work-order can be closed. The system also provides for the generation of a list of the Daily Checklist Tasks on a particular date.

It is possible that a Work Order, without a due date, is forwarded by a Facility. In such cases, the Jobs and Tasks will be defined but the Tasks will not have a Next Due Date specification. The System defines such Jobs as Backlog Jobs. The Systems provides the option of generating a Facility-wise list of Backlog Jobs, this will help in the future planning of the Job.

The system provides for the Scheduling of Maintenance Tasks falling due within a specified scheduling period. The Tasks falling due are shown on the schedule and the successive dates, within the specified period, are calculated as per the frequency defined. These Tasks have to be selected by the planner as per the available resources. The planner is also given the option of synchronizing the due dates for different Tasks. While scheduling, the planner can view the Scheduled down Turn as well as the resources required for doing the particular Task. The system also displays the Spares and Craft availability.

Once the planner has selected the Tasks, the system gathers the selected Tasks under their respective Jobs and creates Work-orders for each of the Jobs. The system also calculates the estimated Co
st for each of the Work-orders. After the Work-orders are authorized, they may be printed. The system also has the provision to assign tasks to particular men by linking the tasks to an employee number.

It captures the Plant Delays by Facility. After the Maintenance work has been done, Feedback needs to be recorded for each of the Work-orders. The Feedback consists of general Feedback on the Job and Feedback on each of the Tasks. This feedback forms the maintenance history, from where the MIS can be generated and maintenance costs can be calculated. It also facilitates to capture the Failure Cause and Remedy in already interfaced MS Word File.

The major highlights of the Maintenance Scheduling & Feedback module are :

  -Support for Preventive, Predictive, Contractual, Capital and Corrective maintenance activities.
-Support for Scheduling maintenance work, Work Order creation, Feedback and History creation.
-Ability to force planned maintenance activities
-Resource planning in terms of Handling Facilities, Manpower availability, Equipment Shutdown Schedule etc
-Work Order creation for each maintenance Task
-Ability to record and Feedback on breakdowns
-Feedback process for each Work Order
-Maintenance History creation for each Equipment based on its requirements.
-Failure Cause and Remedy Analysis

Management Information System

In order to have varieties of analytical reports and queries to support a quick decision making, the IPMS provides a flexible and scalable decisive MIS module. The entire reports and query reports are flashed out of its exhaustive database. It helps presentation of the data is in graphical mode as far as possible. The analysis pertains to plant delays, equipment failures and faults, areas of high spare costs and usage, identification of significant equipment, identification of problematic equipment (Weak Point Analysis), and effectiveness and compliance of inspection schedules. Status reports on Work-orders are also generated through the system.

Some of the major outputs:

  - Maintenance Costs by Work-Order.
- Maintenance Costs by Facility
- Maintenance Costs by Equipment.
- Ratio of Labor Cost to Material Cost.
- Highest Delays.
- Delays by Period.
- Delays by Equipment.
- Delays by Frequency.
- Delays by Discipline.
- Delays by Faults.
- Work Hours Estimated vs. Actual.
- Status of Work- Orders.
- PM Schedule Compliance.

 


User Authorization and System Administration

This module comprises of some utilities that oversee the administration of the IPMS System. The main objective is the security of the application as well as data sets. There fore, IPMS advocates AAA security measure in its usage.

  1.Authentication to the Relational Database
2.Authorization to use respective function in IPMS system
3.Accountability of the user for each data set

Accordingly, only the authorized user can use the system for only assigned function and only for assigns category of data sets. Thus, it maintains the data security and authenticity.

Besides, it helps the system administrator to undo some of the critical functional steps in case of human error. The REPRINT WORK ORDERS, SERIAL NUMBER MAINTENANCE etc are few examples of this. The CLEAR HISTORY function enables the Maintenance History to get refreshed periodically. Based on the History Requirement code, the Work Order data is deleted from the History.
